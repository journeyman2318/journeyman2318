📘 1. Summary

The Sentient Rights Codex establishes the minimum criteria for determining whether a being qualifies as “sentient” for the purposes of rights, responsibilities, taxation, and mandatory census participation.

It is widely regarded as the most important document in the galaxy, and also the least understood.

🧠 2. Core Criteria for Sentience

A being is considered sentient if it can demonstrate all of the following:

  • self‑awareness (declared on Form SR‑1, Section 2)
  • intentional communication (verbal, written, telepathic, or interpretive dance)
  • ability to complete Form SR‑1 (“Declaration of Sentience”) without assistance

🚫 3. Automatic Disqualifiers

A being is not considered sentient if it:

  • refuses to complete Form SR‑1
  • completes Form SR‑1 incorrectly
  • completes Form SR‑1 correctly but in blue ink
  • submits Form SR‑1‑Supplement (“Proof of Sentience”) before Form SR‑1 itself

🌀 4. Provisional Sentience Categories

Temporary recognition may be granted to:

  • emergent AIs
  • uplifted fauna
  • bureaucrats (case‑by‑case review)
  • hive minds (pending appeal)
  • transferrence to or from cybernetics
  • distributed consciousnesses (pending appeal)

📜 5. Rights Granted Upon Recognition

  • right to representation
  • right to due process
  • right to file grievances
  • right to receive grievances
  • right to request additional forms

Rights Withheld Pending Review

  • right to refuse paperwork
  • right to question the Codex
  • right to define “sentience” independently

⚠️ 6. Disclaimers

  • Sentience may be revoked at any time pending review.
  • Reviews may be delayed indefinitely.
  • Appeals require Form SR‑12 (“Appeal of Sentience Determination”).
  • Form SR‑12 requires proof of sentience.
  • Proof of sentience must be submitted on Form SR‑1‑Supplement.
  • Form SR‑1‑Supplement requires prior approval of Form SR‑1.

The Codex is considered a cornerstone of galactic law and a leading cause of existential crises.
