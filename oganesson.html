Oganesson System

Stellar Class: Yellow Supergiant

Habitable Worlds: 3

Primary Identity: Academic & Research Civilization

Flagship Institution: University of Tempes

Overview

The Oganesson System is one of the Triad’s oldest and most stable academic centers, orbiting a massive yellow supergiant whose long, slow burn has allowed uninterrupted scholarly development for millennia. Its three habitable worlds form a coordinated but decentralized research culture, each specializing in a distinct mode of inquiry.

The system’s reputation predates the Wave and survived it largely intact; while other worlds rebuilt, Oganesson simply recalibrated its questions.

Government & Administration

Oganesson is governed by the Tri‑Campus Concord, a cooperative academic council composed of representatives from each habitable world and the University of Tempes. The Concord’s authority is limited by design: it standardizes research ethics, manages interplanetary grants, and oversees shared infrastructure, but avoids interfering with the intellectual autonomy of each world.

Civil governance on each planet is handled locally, typically by elected academic boards or civic councils with strong university representation.

Culture

Oganesson’s culture is defined by scholarly rigor, methodological purity, and a near‑religious respect for inquiry. Debate is a civic pastime. Peer review is a social ritual. Even casual conversation tends to drift toward hypotheses, models, or the latest departmental drama.

Despite this, the system is not elitist. Education is universal, and even non‑academics speak with the precision of trained researchers. The arts flourish here as well, often blending scientific concepts with aesthetic expression.

Economy

The system’s economy is almost entirely research‑driven. Funding flows from:

  • Triad grants
  • Interstellar academic partnerships
  • Licensing of discoveries
  • The Morris‑Alekston Foundation
  • Visiting scholars and long‑term research residencies

Traditional industry exists but is heavily automated and largely invisible.

The Three Research Worlds

Oganesson III — Theoretical Studies

Focus: What might exist.

This world is dedicated to abstract frameworks, mathematical models, hypothetical constructs, and frontier theories. Its scholars explore the boundaries of possibility, often without expectation of immediate application.

  • Cosmological modeling
  • Exotic matter theory
  • Temporal mechanics
  • Hypothetical biology
  • Multidimensional topology

Oganesson III is where ideas are born.

Oganesson IV — Practical Research

Focus: What does exist.

The most grounded of the three worlds, Oganesson IV handles empirical science, experimental validation, and hands‑on engineering. Laboratories, test ranges, and fabrication facilities dominate its surface.

  • Applied physics
  • Material sciences
  • Robotics and automation
  • Environmental systems
  • Medical and biological experimentation

Oganesson IV is where ideas are tested.

Oganesson V — Analytical Research

Focus: What can exist.

This world bridges theory and practice. Its researchers evaluate feasibility, stability, scalability, and long‑term implications. They determine which theoretical constructs can be realized and which practical results can be generalized.

  • Systems analysis
  • Predictive modeling
  • Feasibility studies
  • Ethical and societal impact assessments
  • Cross‑disciplinary synthesis

Oganesson V is where ideas are refined.

University of Tempes

The intellectual heart of the system, located on Oganesson III but with satellite campuses across all three worlds. Tempes is known for:

  • Interplanetary faculty rotations
  • A rigorous cross‑disciplinary curriculum
  • The Tri‑World Thesis requirement, demanding research conducted on all three planets
  • A culture of open debate and public lectures
  • Producing many of the Triad’s most influential thinkers

Tempes is both a university and a cultural anchor — the system’s shared identity.

Honorifics & Academic Titles

Oganesson maintains a set of prestigious, culturally significant titles awarded for exceptional scholarship or service:

  • Doctor — Standard academic mastery
  • Knight of the Triad — Awarded for contributions benefiting all three worlds
  • Chromonaught — Granted to those who expand the boundaries of theoretical understanding
  • Himonionite — Awarded for analytical breakthroughs with system‑wide impact

Interstellar Relations

Oganesson is respected, occasionally resented, and frequently consulted. Its scholars serve as advisors to governments, corporations, and exploratory fleets. The system avoids political entanglements but exerts soft power through intellectual influence.

Travel & Infrastructure

  • High‑capacity interplanetary shuttles link the three worlds in a constant academic circuit.
  • Orbital lecture halls and conference stations host system‑wide symposia.
  • Data relays maintain synchronized research archives across all planets.

The system is optimized for movement of people, ideas, and prototypes.
