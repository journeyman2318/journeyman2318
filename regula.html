🌍 1. System Overview

Regula is the administrative heart of the Tempes Galaxy and the seat of the Central Organized Government (COG) and its enforcement arm, the Central Organized Governance Stewards (COGS).

It is the galaxy’s de facto capital — not by law, but by inevitability. Located near the galactic center, Regula was chosen for symbolic neutrality, logistical convenience, corridor accessibility, and political centrality.

Regula is:

  • crowded
  • loud
  • hyper‑regulated
  • politically charged
  • culturally significant
  • impossible to avoid

Every major corridor route eventually touches Regula. Every citizen’s identity is registered here. Every major decision flows through its bureaucratic machinery.

🏛️ 2. Government & Administration

Regula is not the “official” capital — but it might as well be. The COG operates from here, supported by a vast network of agencies, councils, and oversight bodies.

Key Institutions

The Registry of Sentient Persons
The towering bureaucratic monolith responsible for identity, travel permissions, legal status, and citizenship verification. If your name exists in the galaxy, it exists here.

The Museum of Tempes
The galaxy’s cultural archive, historical vault, and symbolic heart of civilization. Records and artifacts of the entire galaxy reside here.

The Civic Spire
A monumental structure where the COG administrative councils meet — intentionally confusing, a maze of chambers, lifts, and rotating halls.

The Orbital Traffic Authority (OTA)
The most overworked organization in the galaxy. They manage every ship, landing window, corridor arrival, and complaint.

Administrative Culture

Regula’s governance is a labyrinth of:

  • committees
  • subcommittees
  • oversight boards
  • ethics panels
  • review councils
  • procedural audits

Everything takes longer than it should. Everything requires a form. Everything requires a second form.

🏙️ 3. The Capital World: Regula III

Regula III is the system’s primary world — a hyper‑dense, hyper‑regulated metropolis‑planet. Its capital city, Regulus, is a vertical megacity layered in:

  • skywalks
  • transit rails
  • holo‑ads
  • civic towers
  • bureaucratic complexes
  • residential stacks

The city never sleeps. It barely even rests.

District Highlights

Museum District
Heavily patrolled, culturally sacred, home to the Museum of Tempes.

Registry District
Endless lines. Endless paperwork. Endless frustration.

Transit Core
The arrival point for corridor travelers — a chaotic blend of tourism, immigration, and bureaucratic triage.

Civic Heart
Government buildings, public forums, and the Civic Spire.

Lower Strata
Crowded, chaotic, full of vendors, unofficial services, and people who know how to “expedite” paperwork.

The Outer Rings
Where the real work on Regulus happens — far from the heart of the city.

🛰️ 4. Orbital Infrastructure

Regula’s orbit is a ballet of controlled chaos.

Orbital Features

  • Traffic Control Rings
  • Docking Platforms
  • Corridor Arrival Gates
  • Shuttle Transfer Webs
  • Security Checkpoints
  • COGS Patrol Stations

Every ship entering the system is scanned, logged, categorized, and assigned a landing window.

If a crew leaves Regula with a bad report, returning becomes difficult, slow, bureaucratically painful, and politically risky.

Regula remembers.

📑 5. Bureaucracy & Identity Management

Regula is the galaxy’s administrative core. Identity, legality, and citizenship all flow through the Registry of Sentient Persons.

Registry Functions

  • identity creation
  • identity verification
  • travel permissions
  • corridor access stamps
  • legal status updates
  • dispute resolution
  • archival recordkeeping

The Registry is infamous for:

  • long lines
  • contradictory instructions
  • inconsistent signage
  • “temporary” forms that have existed for 200 years

“If it isn’t filed, it isn’t real.”

🧭 6. Cultural Identity

Regula’s people are:

  • proud
  • formal
  • bureaucratically fluent
  • culturally aware
  • slightly condescending toward frontier worlds

They view themselves as the galaxy’s caretakers — the ones who keep civilization running while everyone else complains.

Common Sayings

  • “Regula decides.”
  • “The system works, eventually.”
  • “If you don’t like the rules, file a petition.”

Regula’s culture values order, documentation, procedure, historical preservation, and civic participation. Even children learn how to fill out forms before they learn arithmetic.

🚦 7. Travel, Corridors & COGS Presence

Regula is one of the most heavily trafficked systems in the galaxy.

Corridor Travel

Nearly every major corridor route intersects here. The Transit Core processes millions of arrivals daily.

COGS Presence

COGS maintain:

  • orbital checkpoints
  • customs inspections
  • identity verification

They are polite, professional, and absolutely unyielding.
