🏛️ MUSEUM OF TEMPES — CULTURAL INSTITUTION ENTRY

Registry Classification: MT‑001 / Core Cultural Archive

Status: Fully Operational

Location: Regula Prime — Civic District

Oversight: Regulan Foundation for Civic Harmony

Hazard Rating: Minimal (Administrative)

Administrator: Anne Theresa Quarian

🌐 1. Grand Concourse

The heart of the museum: vaulted ceilings, banners from every Core World, and the soft hum of holo‑projectors. Visitors often describe the space as “a cathedral built to worship history.”

Featured Exhibit — The Founding of Regula Prime

A sweeping holographic mural depicting the first settlement domes rising from the dust. A quiet plaque notes that “the original domes were replaced for safety reasons,” though no records explain why.

⚔️ 2. Hall of Heroic Ages

Sponsored by A‑Z Industries

A curated, triumphant retelling of the galaxy’s most dramatic conflicts — with just enough corporate influence to make historians twitch.

The Heroes of Old Taldori’

A multi‑level diorama of the Battle Against Rala. Holographic heroes freeze mid‑charge, illuminated by shifting magical light.

Plaque: “Historical accuracy certified by the A‑Z Preservation Initiative.”

The Last Stand of the Iron Choir

A reconstructed battlefield showing the final moments of the Iron Choir, a group of cyber‑monks who sang harmonic frequencies to disable enemy war machines.

Visitors can press a button to hear a “recreation” of the Choir’s final chord — a sound suspiciously auto‑tuned.

👑 3. The Xyraxie Retrospective

Sponsored by The Reemus Group

A soft‑focus, apologetic retelling of the Astral Elf empire’s final days.

The Last Days Before the Wave

A serene hologram of Astral Elves tending crystalline vines.

Plaque: “A harmless, sustainable process to preserve their star.” (A quiet lie.)

The Emperor’s Final Appeal

A golden‑lit hologram of the last Emperor delivering a speech.

Plaque: “His plea for peace was tragically ignored by rogue actors.”

🔮 4. Magic & Artifacts — Before the Age of Artificers

A dim, reverent wing filled with floating runes, humming containment fields, and relics from the era when magic was wild, personal, and unpredictable.

The Elemental Crystal

A rotating shard suspended in layered wards.

Plaque: “Purpose unknown. Believed to stabilize elemental energies.”

The Reversal Ring

A golden ring of unknown properties said to change ones luck.

Plaque: “Many fortunes have been swapped by this artifact.”

The Light Treader Boots

A pair of shoes that look like they would be very light and quiet

Plaque: “Confiscated from a sneaky theif”

The Mages Deck

A small cardboard sleeve surrounded by mist.

Plaque: “A legendary divination tool.”

The Undoing Necklace

A simple string of pearls with a small stone at the end.

Plaque: “This artiface when worn, immediatly undoes a persons last action.”

Murder Cutter

A long swors that with glyphs inscribed in red 'ink?'.

Plaque: “Scholars determined the sword was evil, because it told them it was.”

The Geodesic Sphere

A light colored geodesic sphere of unknown origin.

Plaque: “Do Not Touch.”

The Boots of Bliding Speed

A pair of red rubber boots with Go Faster striped painted on them.

Plaque: “Some people move too fast for their own good.”

The Saviors Shield

A round shield painted red a white.

Plaque: “A magical shield said to save adventures.”

The Wand of Unfinished Spells

A slender wand that occasionally emits half‑formed glyphs.

Plaque: “Recovered from a pre‑Artificer workshop. Known to cast spells that stop mid‑effect.”

🧠 5. Before Photonics — The Multitronic Era

Cooler lighting, polished metal housings, and the faint hum of ancient processors still running on archival power.

The MISTI Line

A sleek amber‑glowing AI core.

Plaque: “The pinnacle of Multitronic design — adaptive, resilient, and capable of emotional modeling.”

The Vespera‑9 Logic Array (new)

A crystalline lattice of interwoven circuits.

Plaque: “Designed to predict stellar drift. Decommissioned after it began predicting ‘unavoidable tragedies.’”

🧬 6. Extinct Species Conservatory

A lush, climate‑controlled biome filled with holographic fauna, preserved remains, and speculative reconstructions.

The Glassback Strider (new)

A towering insectoid creature reconstructed from partial fossils.

The Whispering Reef

A preserved coral‑like organism that emits faint harmonic tones.

🛰️ 7. The Age of Exploration

The First Jump Beacon (new)

A battered, ancient beacon core.

The Cartographer’s Folly

A star map riddled with contradictory routes.

🧰 8. Artifact Preparation & Conservation

Not generally open to the public — but referenced in museum literature as the “behind‑the‑scenes heart of preservation.”

Ask your tour guide if the Conservation room is available for touring. If there is time, you may get a peek!

  • “Visitors may hear diagnostic tones during maintenance cycles.”
  • “Please do not be alarmed.”

⭐ 9. Your Galaxy

A massive rotating hologram of the Tempes Galaxy dominates the hall. Each major system glows with a soft pulse.

The Triad Worlds

A polished, optimistic display highlighting the economic and political backbone of the Core.

The Outer Corridor Systems (new)

A quieter section showing the worlds that maintain the fragile supply lines beyond the Core.

🌊 10. The Wave and Its Destructive Path

The First Collapse (new)

A holographic recreation of the moment the Wave destabilized the first known system.

The Survivors’ Network

A hopeful display showing the systems that adapted, fortified, or rebuilt after the Wave.

🌫️ 11. Cuprum & Plumbia — The Heavy Flux

The Cuprum Drift

A looping projection of the system’s last known stable configuration.

Plumbia’s Vanishing Moons (new)

A mesmerizing display showing the moons of Plumbia phasing in and out of visibility.

🌾 12. Hygenium & Argentum — The Food and the Frontier

Hygenium: The Galactic Breadbasket

A serene display of agricultural domes and irrigation satellites.

Argentum: The Frontier Forge

A stark, angular exhibit showcasing desert outposts and pale skies.

🏛️ 13. The Regula Wing

A celebration of order, oversight, and the comforting weight of bureaucracy.

1. Your Government at Work

Exhibit temporarily closed for scheduled compliance review.

2. The Registry of Sentient Beings

A curated collection of approved — and disapproved — forms of sentient life.

3. The Corridor Transportation Administration

Keeping the galaxy moving — safely, slowly, and with proper documentation.

4. The Prides of the Fleet

A celebration of Regula’s most iconic enforcement and oversight vessels.
