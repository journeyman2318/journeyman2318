🌍 1. System Overview

Cuprum was once an uninhabited, barren world selected by A‑Z Industries as a remote testing ground for:

  • experimental energy systems
  • large‑scale industrial prototypes
  • high‑risk material science
  • classified megastructure trials

Its isolation made it ideal. Its emptiness made it expendable.

Today, none of that matters. Cuprum is gone — sealed inside a shifting, unstable mass of Heavy Flux.

⚠️ 2. Heavy Flux Encasement

Cuprum is completely encased within a dense, semi‑physical, semi‑energetic Flux shell that originated from its counterpart system, Plumbia.

Observed Properties

  • impenetrable to sensors
  • impenetrable to spelljamming
  • impenetrable to corridor entry
  • unreadable by all known methods
  • absorptive of active scans
  • spatially distortive
  • destabilizing to navigation systems

The phenomenon is not expanding, but it reacts to proximity — a slow, deliberate stirring, as if aware of intrusion.

COG Classification: “Absolute Hazard Zone — DO NOT APPROACH.”

🧪 3. A‑Z Industries Legacy

Before the Wave, Cuprum served as one of A‑Z Industries’ most secretive test sites.

Fragmented records indicate:

  • large‑scale energy experiments
  • high‑risk industrial reactors
  • prototype megastructure components

Most documentation is redacted, corrupted, or missing.

The encasement of Cuprum in Heavy Flux is rumored to be:

  • a direct result of A‑Z experimentation,
  • an indirect consequence of the Wave,
  • or something else entirely.

No consensus exists. No evidence remains. No one knows for certain.

🛰️ 4. Current State

Cuprum is:

  • unreachable
  • unreadable
  • uninhabitable
  • unobservable
  • unchanging

The system is effectively erased — preserved only as a silent, swirling sphere of Flux.

No ships approach. No probes return. No signals escape.

The Triad has abandoned all attempts at study.

🛑 5. COG Advisory

DO NOT APPROACH

The Triad’s warnings cite:

  • severe navigational instability
  • sensor blindness
  • gravitational shear
  • temporal drift at the Flux boundary
  • multiple lost vessels
  • no rescue authorization

Approaching Cuprum is considered an act of self‑destruction.
