DR. ELANA REGAS, H — PERSONAL LOGS
Entry 1 — Day 3
A‑Z Industries has built us a wonderful station. It has a strange name though — “Gruyere.” They’ve thrown so many credits at this place. It is amazing.

“Stop the Wave.” That’s our one directive. Money is no object, they say. Apparently not — this station has the latest tech in every corridor. And they put it around Oganesson, of all places.

All the scientists we need for collaboration. The raw Aurum deposits practically at our doorstep. Kalum’s tech sector right next door. This should be a piece of cake.

The generator should last for centuries. The new SCOTI AI is a bit sluggish, but friendly enough. I can work on that in my downtime. My friend at Kalum mentioned something about integrating his photonic projectors into AI matrices… maybe I’ll try that once we’re stable.
Entry 2 — Day 41
Containment Attempt #7 failed today. Same pattern as the last three: the graviton wave collapses the field before we can stabilize the harmonic envelope.

We increased the Aurum lattice density, but the wave simply ignores the barrier. It’s like trying to stop a hurricane with a bedsheet.

Crew morale is… mixed. Some are frustrated. Some are excited. Some are terrified, though they won’t say it out loud.

SCOTI keeps offering “encouraging messages,” which is sweet, but the timing is terrible. He congratulated us on “a statistically significant improvement” while the lab was still smoking.

We’ll try again tomorrow.
Entry 3 — Day 78
Another failure. This one was worse. The containment field didn’t just collapse — it inverted. We lost two auxiliary generators and half the lab sensors.

We’re running out of ideas. The wave isn’t behaving like energy. It’s behaving like intention.

I’ve started dreaming about it. Not nightmares — more like… impressions. Shapes. Pathways. A sense of motion without direction.

I’m not superstitious, but I’m beginning to think we’re missing something fundamental. Something about the nature of space itself.
Entry 4 — Day 103
I had the dream again. This time I woke with a phrase in my mind: “Fold the path, not the wave.”

It sounds ridiculous, but it led to a breakthrough.

We’ve been trying to contain the wave — to trap it. But what if we don’t need to stop it at all? What if we give it a path that loops back into itself?

A Spatial Flexure. A rift connecting two points in space… except instead of point A to point B, we turn the rift inward. A closed loop. An infinite corridor the wave can flow through forever.

The math checks out. The theory is sound.

We just don’t have the power. Not even close.

I’ll need to contact A‑Z. If anyone can get us the energy source we need, it’s them.
Entry 5 — Day 142
The flexure generator is complete. It’s beautiful — elegant, simple, terrifying.

A‑Z responded to my request. They said, “Give us some time. We will have a power source soon.”

They didn’t elaborate. They never do.

But if they deliver what they promised… this might actually work.

For the first time in weeks, I feel hopeful.
Entry 6 — Day 167
One good thing has come from all this spare time. Dr. Light — yes, that’s really his name, and yes, it’s painfully appropriate — brought his photonic emitters to the station, along with an enhanced AI core based on some MISTI system prototype. Very hush‑hush, he said.

We integrated the emitters into SCOTI’s matrix today. The improvement was immediate. SCOTI can now almost run the station himself. He’s still polite to a fault, but he’s learning.

It’s… comforting, actually. Having someone — something — to talk to while we wait.
Entry 7 — Day 189
My contact at A‑Z finally responded. They have a power source, and it’s en route. “Shortly,” they said.

Not a moment too soon. The Wave keeps coming closer. It skirts the system like a predator testing the fence. Good for research, yes, but it scares the devil out of me.

We’re ready for the generator. We’re ready for the power. We’re ready to try.
Entry 8 — Day 214
No sign of the transport yet. They should have been here days ago.

I’ve sent three messages to my A‑Z contact. No response.

SCOTI assures me the comms array is functioning normally. I wish that made me feel better.
Entry 9 — Day 301
It’s been months since my last contact with A‑Z. No sign of the transport. No updates. Nothing.

The generator is useless without the power source. The Wave keeps skirting the area, closer each time.

I don’t know how much longer we can wait. The crew is restless. Some are afraid to admit it, but I see it in their eyes.
Entry 10 — Day 327
I’ve made the call to evacuate the station. It wasn’t an easy decision, but I can’t justify keeping the crew here any longer.

I will stay behind to monitor the systems as long as I can. Someone has to keep the simulations running. Someone has to watch the Wave.

SCOTI offered to “provide emotional support.” I told him that wouldn’t be necessary. He seemed… disappointed.
Entry 11 — Year 4
This will be my last entry. It has been a while.

Every simulation. Every computation. Every single calculation says the same thing: the Flexure would work — if we had enough power. But the amount we need is equivalent to a small star.

I don’t know how A‑Z supposedly solved that problem. I suppose I’ll never know, since the transport never came.

I leave this plane knowing I did what I could. I never stopped trying.

I’ve programmed the sensors to ignore my life signs in three hours. I’m telling SCOTI I’m finally leaving the station. He will see my life signs vanish from the sensors. Hopefully… an AI will do what is logical.

This is Dr. Elana Regas, signing off.
