🜂 1. System Overview

Argentum is a remote, sparsely populated frontier system located far beyond the stable FLASH Gate network. For centuries, the region has been considered:

  • too distant for reliable corridor travel
  • too unstable for consistent spelljamming
  • too isolated to maintain a Galactic Database uplink

As a result, Argentum has effectively fallen out of the galaxy’s shared memory. No COGS presence. No oversight. No recognized jurisdiction.

The COG’s official stance remains unchanged: “Argentum is not a recognized jurisdiction. Travel is strongly discouraged.”

The system’s only inhabited world, Argentum II, persists in a state of perpetual frontier instability, shaped by scarcity, isolation, and the lingering scars of the Wave.

🤠 2. Frontier Conditions & Sociopolitical Climate

Argentum is widely regarded as the Wild West of the Tempes Galaxy — a world where law, order, and interstellar governance never returned after the Wave.

Key Characteristics

  • minimal infrastructure
  • scattered, self‑sufficient settlements
  • barter‑based economies
  • local warlords, sheriffs, and militias
  • high rates of violence and territorial disputes
  • no Triad presence or recognized law enforcement
  • no reliable communication with the rest of the galaxy

Spelljamming is technically possible, but:

  • currents are unstable
  • navigation is unreliable
  • the Wave left deep scars in local space
  • most pilots refuse to attempt entry

Those who do often do not return.

Argentum’s culture is shaped by survivalism, local authority, and a pervasive belief that the galaxy has forgotten them — or chosen to.

📡 3. Database Isolation

Argentum has been cut off from the Galactic Database for centuries. The system’s uplink infrastructure collapsed during the early Wave Era and was never restored.

Consequences

  • no updated census
  • no verified maps
  • no official records
  • no Triad monitoring
  • no FLASH Gate access
  • no corridor stabilization
  • no reliable data synchronization

All modern information comes from:

  • rumor
  • hearsay
  • unverified survivor accounts
  • rare merchant logs
  • emergency transmissions from ships that never made it back

The Database classifies Argentum as “Data‑Dark.”

⚠️ 4. Hazard Advisory

COG WARNING: DO NOT APPROACH

Argentum is flagged for:

  • extreme lawlessness
  • unregulated weapons
  • unpredictable spelljammer currents
  • high casualty rates among visitors
  • multiple missing‑ship reports
  • anomalous temporal readings (see Section 6)

The COGS do not patrol the system. No rescue operations are authorized.

No insurance provider recognizes travel to Argentum as a covered activity.

🌍 5. Environmental & Planetary Conditions

Argentum’s surface is harsh and unforgiving:

  • thin atmosphere
  • wide temperature swings
  • dust‑laden winds
  • long‑distance visibility distorted by heat shimmer
  • settlements built from scavenged materials and pre‑Wave ruins

The night sky is unnervingly clear — stars visible even at midday — a reminder of how thin the veil between Argentum and wildspace has become.

Localized gravitational anomalies have been reported, though no formal study has been completed.

🕒 6. Temporal Distortion Zone

Classification: Unexplained Phenomenon

COG Advisory: DO NOT APPROACH

Deep within Argentum’s southern hemisphere lies an isolated region where time does not behave consistently.

Reported Effects

  • time dilation
  • time compression
  • temporal “skips”
  • memory discontinuities
  • inconsistent aging
  • contradictory eyewitness accounts
  • loss of chronological continuity

These effects remain poorly documented because:

  • no scientific team has returned with complete data
  • most who enter do not return at all
  • survivors report fragmented, unreliable memories
  • instruments fail or return contradictory readings

The COG’s official designation: “Temporal Hazard Zone — Entry Prohibited.”

Unofficially, the region is whispered about across frontier settlements — a place where time “bends,” “loops,” or “forgets.”

🏚️ 7. Settlement Patterns & Local Governance

Argentum’s settlements are small, scattered, and fiercely independent.

Common Settlement Types

  • fortified homesteads
  • mining camps
  • trade outposts
  • militia‑controlled towns
  • abandoned pre‑Wave facilities

Governance

There is no unified authority. Power is held by:

  • warlords
  • sheriffs
  • clan leaders
  • charismatic preachers
  • mercenary captains

Justice is local, inconsistent, and often violent.

🌐 8. Travel & Navigation

Spelljamming

Possible, but dangerous. Currents are unstable and prone to sudden collapse.

Corridor Travel

No active corridors reach Argentum. The nearest FLASH Gate is too distant to support safe transit.

Common Pilot Warnings

  • “Don’t trust your instruments.”
  • “If the stars shift, turn back.”
  • “If you hear echoes, you’re already too deep.”

🔭 9. Historical Summary

  • Pre‑Wave: Remote but stable frontier world.
  • Early Wave Era: Corridor collapse isolates system.
  • Mid‑Wave Era: Settlements fragment; lawlessness rises.
  • Modern Era: System considered lost; temporal anomaly discovered.
