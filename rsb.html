⭐ REGISTRY OF SENTIENT BEINGS — ORGANIZATIONAL ENTRY

Registry Code: RSB‑PRM

Classification: Pan‑Galactic Administrative Monolith

Oversight: Central Organized Government, Department of Mandatory Compliance

Status: Permanently Operational

Headquarters: A building so large it has its own weather

Hazard Rating: Bureaucratic Catastrophic

Jurisdiction: Everything that breathes, thinks, or might someday do either

Primary Function: Identity, Sentience, and Compliance Administration

1. Overview

The Registry of Sentient Beings is the galaxy’s foremost authority on:

  • who counts
  • who doesn’t
  • who used to count but no longer does
  • who insists they count but the paperwork disagrees

The Registry’s official purpose is to “ensure the orderly classification of all sapient entities.”

Its unofficial purpose is to “ensure no one ever completes a form on the first try.”

The Registry’s motto, displayed in 4‑meter‑tall letters above the main atrium, reads:
“Sentience is a privilege. Paperwork is eternal.”

2. Core Functions

Identity Verification Bureau

Responsible for:

  • issuing identity tokens
  • revoking identity tokens
  • losing identity tokens
  • insisting the lost token was never issued
  • issuing a replacement token that contradicts the first

Guiding principle: “If you cannot prove you are you, we cannot help you until you do.”

Transit & Mobility Permissions Office

Controls all intersystem travel. Services include:

  • approving travel
  • denying travel
  • approving travel but marking it “pending denial”
  • denying travel but marking it “provisionally approved”

Travelers must complete Form 19‑T, which includes the question:
“Do you intend to remain yourself during transit?”

Species Rights & Classification Division

Determines which species qualify as “sentient.” Common outcomes:

  • Sentient
  • Provisionally Sentient
  • Sentient Pending Review
  • Sentient But Not In That Way
  • Non‑Sentient But Very Enthusiastic
  • Reclassified Due to Clerical Error

Appeals require Form 12‑K, Form 12‑K‑Supplement A, and Form 12‑K‑Supplement A‑Errata.

Behavioral Compliance & Oversight Unit

Duties include:

  • issuing Behavioral Advisory Status
  • tracking “mood anomalies”
  • maintaining the Non‑Cooperative Entities List
  • sending strongly worded letters

Motto: “We are not angry. We are disappointed.”

Census & Population Analytics Directorate

Runs the galaxy‑wide census. Notable achievements:

  • counting everyone
  • counting everyone again
  • counting everyone a third time because the second count disagreed with the first
  • discovering “ghost entries” that insist they are alive

The Holographic Census Sphere occasionally displays numbers that no one can explain.

3. Physical Headquarters

The Registry’s central building is a colossal, species‑neutral structure designed to make all visitors feel equally insignificant.

  • a lobby large enough to host weather patterns
  • a “take a number” system that issues numbers in non‑sequential order
  • a help desk staffed by someone who has been on break for 412 years
  • signage that contradicts itself (“LEFT WING →” and “← LEFT WING”)
  • a waiting area where time appears to slow down (the Registry denies this)

The building hums softly, as if thinking.

4. Visitor Experience

Upon arrival, visitors are instructed to take a number. Approximate wait times vary from 10 minutes to 2.54 years.

If called to a window without the correct forms, visitors must start over.

Common reports include:

  • being sent to “Window 7” which does not exist
  • being told to “come back yesterday”
  • being asked to provide “proof of non‑duplication in triplicate”
  • being thanked for their patience before they have shown any

7. Known Issues & Controversies

  • Misfiled identities resulting in two people being legally the same person
  • Sentience appeals that have been “under review” since before the species evolved
  • Ghost entries that file their own paperwork
  • A rumored Sub‑Registry for “entities of uncertain ontology”
  • The building occasionally rearranges its hallways

The Registry denies all allegations, including the ones it filed against itself.
