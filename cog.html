📘 1. Overview

The Central Organized Government (COG) is the galaxy’s primary governing authority, responsible for maintaining order, enforcing compliance, and ensuring that no one, anywhere, ever forgets to file the correct paperwork.

COG’s stated mission is to “provide unified governance across all recognized territories.” Its actual mission is widely believed to be “preventing the galaxy from collapsing under the weight of its own contradictions.”

COG’s motto, etched into the marble of the Administrative Spire, reads: “Unity Through Structure. Structure Through Forms.”

Before the Wave, the galaxy existed in a state of laissez‑faire drift. Each system governed itself — or didn’t — according to its own traditions, whims, or complete lack of interest in governance.

The only exception was the Triad Worlds — Kalium, Oganesson, and Aurum — which maintained a tightly knit alliance built on shared scientific advancement, resource exchange, and a mutual belief that everyone else was doing things wrong.

This loose, comfortable disorganization lasted until the Xyraxis star collapsed.

⚙️ 2. Core Functions

Executive Coordination Directorate

Oversees all high‑level governance decisions. Responsibilities include:

  • issuing mandates
  • revising mandates
  • issuing clarifications to revised mandates
  • denying that the original mandates ever existed
  • approving emergency measures that create new emergencies

Guiding principle: “If it wasn’t documented, it didn’t happen.”

Interstellar Compliance Bureau

Duties include:

  • conducting compliance audits
  • conducting audits of the audits
  • issuing fines for improper audit preparation
  • maintaining the Galactic Non‑Compliance Index
  • sending “friendly reminders” that are not friendly

Unofficial motto: “We don’t enforce the rules. The rules enforce themselves.”

Department of Mandatory Forms & Documentation

The largest department in COG. Responsible for:

  • creating forms
  • revising forms
  • merging forms into larger forms
  • splitting forms into smaller forms
  • losing forms
  • denying that forms were ever submitted

This department is the primary source of the Registry of Sentient Beings’ paperwork ecosystem.

Council of Systemic Governance (COGS)

A rotating council of representatives from major systems. Tasks include:

  • debating policy
  • debating the debate rules
  • debating whether the debate is in quorum
  • passing resolutions that require additional resolutions to interpret

COGS is widely regarded as “symbolic,” though no one agrees on what it symbolizes.

Galactic Infrastructure & Logistics Authority

Manages:

  • FLASH gate networks
  • corridor maintenance
  • transit taxation
  • emergency rerouting
  • the annual “Corridor Congestion Forecast,” which is always wrong

Responsible for the infamous “Triad Arcway Improvement Project,” now in its 312th year.

🏛️ 3. Physical Headquarters

COG’s headquarters is located on Regula Prime, within the Administrative Spire, a structure so tall it requires its own orbital clearance.

Notable features include:

  • a lobby with a 12‑hour queue on quiet days
  • elevators that operate on a “probabilistic schedule”
  • a central chamber where laws are drafted, debated, and occasionally misplaced
  • a climate‑controlled archive containing every form ever created (and several that haven’t been created yet)

The Spire emits a low hum, believed to be either the ventilation system or the sound of governance itself.

📑 5. Relationship With the Registry of Sentient Beings

COG provides oversight, funding, and mandates to the Registry. The Registry provides forms, data, and existential headaches to COG.

Their relationship is described as:

  • “symbiotic”
  • “co‑dependent”
  • “a paperwork singularity”

COG denies all allegations of mismanagement, including those filed by COG.

🌌 6. Origins & Formation

Before the Wave, the galaxy operated under a “let it be” philosophy. Systems minded their own business, borders were optional, and governance was mostly a suggestion.

The collapse of the Xyraxis star changed everything. The resulting Wave tore through systems, destabilizing matter, erasing worlds, and ignoring every political boundary ever drawn.

The Triad Worlds attempted to coordinate a response, but even they couldn’t manage a crisis of this scale alone.

A de facto governing body formed on Regula, chosen for its central location, infrastructure, and abundance of empty office space. What began as a temporary crisis council slowly ossified into something permanent.

Expansion

Over decades and centuries, the council expanded:

  • committees
  • subcommittees
  • oversight boards
  • advisory panels
  • emergency councils
  • councils to oversee the emergency councils

Each new crisis created a new department. Each new department created new forms. Each new form required new oversight.

By the time the Wave stabilized into a predictable (if still catastrophic) phenomenon, COG had become bloated, labyrinthine, self‑perpetuating, impossible to fully map, and absolutely convinced of its own necessity.

Different branches operate independently until their actions collide — at which point committees form, investigations begin, forms are issued, blame is assigned, and nothing is resolved.

COG can barely govern itself, but it remains extremely effective at demanding that everyone else follow procedure.

⚠️ 7. Known Issues & Controversies

  • Mandates that contradict earlier mandates
  • Systems classified as “provisionally governed” for centuries
  • A backlog of unresolved appeals dating to pre‑Wave eras
  • The “Great Compliance Scandal,” which no one is allowed to discuss
  • A rumored “Shadow Council” that definitely does not exist
  • The Administrative Spire occasionally rearranges its floors

COG denies all allegations, including the ones it filed against itself.

📜 9. Historical Summary

  • Pre‑Wave: Galaxy governed loosely; Triad Worlds maintain structured alliance.
  • Xyraxis Collapse: Wave forms; laissez‑faire governance collapses.
  • Early Wave Era: Crisis council forms on Regula.
  • Triad Consolidation: COG becomes central authority for all recognized systems.
  • Mid‑Wave Era: COG expands emergency powers; bureaucracy metastasizes.
  • Modern Era: COG oversees governance, compliance, infrastructure, and the Registry.

COG describes its history as “stable.” Historians describe it as “inevitable.”
